About

Engineer by training,
Leader by experience

🎓 Ship to the Shore

Graduating from Korea Maritime & Ocean University in 2010 with a Bachelor's in Marine System Engineering — a university renowned not only for academic rigor but for its regimental structure and military-style discipline that forges leadership character from day one. That foundation of order, responsibility, and mission-driven focus has defined every role I've held since. I began my career at sea as a Marine Engineer with Hanjin Shipping, operating container vessels up to 13,100 TEU across worldwide oceans.

From the ocean to the shore, I entered the shipbuilding industry as a Technical Manager at NSCA Engine Service & Hyundai Global Service — serving the very heart of the fleet: the engine. I managed the full spectrum of 2-stroke & 4-stroke engines across every type — Diesel, dual-fuel, and gas engines — providing comprehensive technical support to offshore and onshore power plant customers worldwide.

At Crowley Maritime, I expanded beyond the engine room into fleet-level strategy — supporting vessel asset management across the organization and leading retrofit and modification projects as part of the project engineering team. This dual exposure to both Asset Management and Project Engineering gave me a complete view of the full vessel lifecycle.

Now at Caterpillar as Lead Project Manager within the Oil & Gas Marine division, I lead the charge on marine electrification — specifically hybrid vessel projects powered by marine battery systems. I drive the full project lifecycle for next-generation power solutions, integrating Cat engines with battery systems, inverters, and motors for tomorrow's marine fleet.

As Korea's shipbuilding technology and maritime capabilities grow in influence across the U.S. market, bilingual fluency in English and Korean has become a strategic differentiator — not a soft skill, but a business-critical capability. I serve as a direct bridge between Korean engineering teams, OEM manufacturers, and American customers and stakeholders. This linguistic and cultural fluency has already delivered measurable value, and as U.S.-Korea maritime collaboration deepens, that contribution will only grow.
